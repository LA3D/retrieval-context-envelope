Data appendix

Rank tables, gold-only comparisons, and methodology notes for the claims on the landing page.

Modified

July 11, 2026

This page is the empirical backing for the landing page. It is versioned alongside the code that produces the numbers. If a value changes on re-run, the value here changes, and the commit history records why. The scaled chain-on-21k comparison is the one open cell; everything else is measured.

Setup

The corpus

The evaluation runs on MuSiQue (Trivedi et al., 2022), a multi-hop question-answering benchmark constructed by chaining single-hop questions from SQuAD, T-REx, Zero Shot RE, Natural Questions, and MLQA. The version used here is the answerable split (2-, 3-, and 4-hop questions with grounded supporting evidence).

  • Full corpus: 21,100 candidate paragraphs (the union of every question’s supporting passages plus MuSiQue’s distractor pool).
  • Gold-only subset: 281 chunks, restricted to the paragraphs that contain the supporting evidence for the 120-question stratified sample. This isolates the retrieval-ranking problem from the recall problem.
  • Held-out test set: 90 questions, 30 each at 2-, 3-, and 4-hop.

Rank distribution

Where do the supporting chunks land?

The metric is g_i: the 1-indexed rank, within the corpus’s cosine ranking, of the i-th-closest gold chunk for a question. g_1 is the gold chunk the retriever ranks highest; g_4 is the deepest of a 4-hop question’s four gold chunks. Cells report median [p75] (max) over 30 questions.

Dense RAG on the full 21,100-chunk corpus

hops g1 g2 g3 g4
2-hop 1 [2] (8) 143 [786] (5042)
3-hop 1 [2] (16) 13 [47] (1138) 101 [522] (5775)
4-hop 1 [3] (129) 45 [182] (1388) 268 [1049] (2394) 1796 [3027] (9572)

Dense RAG on the 281-chunk gold-only corpus

hops g1 g2 g3 g4
2-hop 1 [1] (1) 3 [17] (79)
3-hop 1 [1] (1) 2 [3] (15) 5 [12] (77)
4-hop 1 [1] (3) 3 [5] (37) 7 [21] (45) 30 [65] (144)

Reading the two tables together makes the shape unmistakable. g_1 sits at rank 1 in both corpora, at every hop count: the chunk that directly matches the question’s named entity is trivial for cosine. The deeper gold chunks are the ones that move. On 281 chunks, g_4 for a 4-hop question sits at median 30, entirely usable. On 21,100 chunks, the same g_4 sinks to median 1,796 with a p75 of 3,027. First hop is not the problem. The problem is every gold chunk after the first, scaled against realistic distractors.

K = 30 coverage on the full 21k

all-gold@K is the fraction of questions whose every gold chunk survives inside the top-K.

K % corpus all-gold
5 0.0 % 3.3 %
30 0.1 % 22.2 %
100 0.5 % 32.2 %
200 0.9 % 47.8 %
500 2.4 % 54.4 %
1000 4.7 % 65.6 %
2000 9.5 % 76.7 %
5000 23.7 % 93.3 %

You would need to feed the language model roughly 2,000 chunks to cover every gold chunk for 77 % of questions. This is what “dense RAG is broken on multi-hop at scale” means as a concrete claim.

What K = 10 looks like

K = 10 is the practical production cutoff once you factor in lost-in-the-middle (Liu et al., 2024). The rank medians above translate to K = 10 coverage as follows:

  • 2-hop g2: median 143. Landing inside K = 10 for roughly 10 to 15 % of questions.
  • 3-hop g3: median 101. Landing inside K = 10 for roughly 5 % of questions.
  • 4-hop g4: median 1,796. Effectively never inside K = 10.

So at the K the attention curve actually lets you use, dense RAG hands the language model one gold chunk of four for a 4-hop question, and misses the bridge chunk for the vast majority of 2-hop questions. Turning K up past 30 does not fix it, because 30 is where the middle-of-context attention penalty starts eating into answer quality faster than recall improves.

Comparison

Gold-only: dense vs graph-informed

The gold-only subset lets us compare two retrieval strategies without the recall confound. Both strategies see the same 281 paragraphs; the only difference is how they rank them.

  • Dense: cosine similarity between question embedding and paragraph embeddings (mpnet, L2-normalized), single-shot.
  • Chain v5c2: bipartite chunk-entity graph with structural occurrence-count edges (no chunk-chunk, no entity-entity). Random walk with restart, c = 0.15, alpha = 0.5, two-channel softmax seeding on question-chunk-text and question-entity-label.
hops metric g1 g2 g3 g4
2-hop dense 1 [1] (1) 3 [17] (79)
2-hop chain 1 [1] (1) 2 [3] (54)
3-hop dense 1 [1] (1) 2 [3] (15) 5 [12] (77)
3-hop chain 1 [1] (2) 2 [2] (24) 4 [5] (71)
4-hop dense 1 [1] (3) 3 [5] (37) 7 [21] (45) 30 [65] (144)
4-hop chain 1 [1] (3) 2 [3] (33) 5 [12] (77) 19 [29] (206)

Three things fall out of this table:

  • g_1 is a tie. Dense cosine trivially finds the seed chunk; the walk adds nothing on the first hop, because the first hop is not the problem.
  • The chain wins on bridge chunks, more so the deeper the hop. 2-hop g_2 p75 drops from 17 to 3. 4-hop g_4 p75 drops from 65 to 29. The chunks that dense retrieval buries because they do not lexically match the question are the chunks the entity-bridge walk keeps near the top.
  • One reversal. The chain’s worst-case 4-hop g_4 (max 206) is worse than dense’s (max 144). Walks usually lift deep chunks but occasionally misroute mass and bury a single chunk worse than dense does. This is a known failure mode of RWR, not a metric artifact.

The chain’s advantage concentrates on the deepest hop, which is exactly where the trajectory-length argument predicts it should: the walk’s reach scales with (1 - c) / c, and a low restart probability sends mass further into the graph.

Open

The one open comparison

The 21k-scale chain-vs-dense comparison is the still-open cell. Dense RAG on 21k is the collapsed baseline (g_4 median 1,796, all-gold@30 22 %). The chain has been run on the 281 gold-only corpus (g_4 median 19). The chain on the full 21k corpus is deferred, blocked on a distillation step estimated at roughly $300 in compute. The numbers here will be updated as soon as that run lands.

The prediction to falsify: at 21k, the chain keeps every g_i inside K = 30, closing the all-gold@30 gap from 22 % well into the 60s or higher. If it does not, the operator-splitting story on the landing page needs an accounting.

Methodology

How the graph is built

Chain v5c2 uses a bipartite chunk-entity graph. Concretely:

  1. Entity extraction over each paragraph, producing a canonical entity label per mention.
  2. Chunk-entity edges weighted by the count of the entity’s occurrences inside the chunk. No chunk-chunk edges. No entity-entity edges.
  3. Two transition temperatures at the walk step: T_entity = 0.05 when transitioning from entity to chunk, T_chunk = 0.05 when transitioning from chunk to entity. Both act as softmax sharpness.
  4. Two-channel seeding at query time: the question is embedded and softmaxed against chunk texts and entity labels separately, then the two seed distributions are mixed with alpha = 0.5.

The walk is a personalized PageRank with restart c = 0.15. Full ranking over all corpus chunks is read off the stationary distribution; no top-K cutoff is applied at ranking time.

For projects that already ship linked notes (a wiki with [[link]] syntax and typed frontmatter), the entity layer is unnecessary because the concept graph is the wiki graph itself. This is the setting where the approach is cheapest to deploy.

Caveats

What we are not claiming

  • This does not replace dense retrieval. Dense similarity seeds the walk. The claim is that walk-informed re-ranking closes the gap on hops dense-alone misses.
  • The 21k chain result is not in yet. The 21k dense collapse is measured; the 21k chain result is projected, not observed. Do not cite the chain at scale until the deferred run completes.
  • This has not been evaluated on all corpora. MuSiQue is a stress test built for multi-hop; results here do not automatically generalize to single-hop domains or to corpora where evidence is not graph-structured.
  • GraphRAG and community-based methods are related. (Edge et al., 2024) Hierarchical community summaries are a different way to solve the same envelope-assembly problem. Which method wins depends on corpus structure; both beat plain dense at scale.
